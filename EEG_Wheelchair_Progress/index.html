E-WHEELCHAIR V3

HIERARCHICAL GAZE-FREE BCI NAVIGATION

A fully embedded, battery-operated, hands-free wheelchair system designed for high-level spinal cord injury patients. Classifying Motor Imagery (MI) dynamics for navigational autonomy.

PHASE: INTEGRATION

The Mission

Problem: Standard joystick controls are unusable for patients with Locked-in Syndrome or ALS, and existing BCI solutions rely on heavy external laptops, preventing real-world mobility.


Goal: Engineer a system that runs entirely on embedded hardware (Raspberry Pi 5), does not occupy the user's visual channel (Gaze-free), and handles the low Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR) inherent in wearable EEG.

The Tech Stack

A. Signal Acquisition (The Nerves)

  • Node: Custom OpenBCI Cyton Clone (ADS1299 ADC)
  • Electrodes: Dry Ag/AgCl at C3, Cz, C4 (Motor Cortex)
  • Headset: Custom Ultracortex (PLA Printed)
  • Connectivity: Bluetooth HC-06 (Galvanic Isolation)
  • Power: Isolated 3.7V Li-Ion

B. Hierarchical Computing (The Brain)

  • Host: Raspberry Pi 5 (8GB) - AI & Filtering
  • Stack: Linux, MNE-Python, TensorFlow
  • Accelerator: Google Coral Edge TPU
  • Controller: Arduino Mega 2560 (Watchdog & Motor PWM)

C. Actuation (The Body)

  • Drive: 48V BO3 High-Torque Hub Motors
  • Steering: DC Gear Active Steering (No skid-steer jerk)
  • Drivers: BTS7960 High-Current H-Bridges
  • Chassis: Custom "Pegboard" modular electrical box

D. Algorithms (The Mind)

  • Filter: 5th-Order Butterworth (8-30 Hz)
  • Features: Common Spatial Pattern (CSP)
  • Classifier: Linear Discriminant Analysis (LDA)
  • Safety: >500ms Latency Safe-Stop

Design Decisions (FAQ)

Why Reject NI USB-6009?

Although available, the NI USB-6009 fails two critical requirements:

  • Gain Mismatch: Lacks PGA for micro-volt EEG signals (SNR ~0).
  • Safety Violation: Wired USB breaks Galvanic Isolation. Bluetooth provides a necessary air-gap barrier against 48V motor spikes.
